Overview

This website goes over seven Instructional Design Principles originally presented by Clark & Mayer, 2016. There are activities throughout to help you get familiar with application of these principles.

To navigate, click on the buttons along the top to go to the principle you'd like to review.

Practice

The following is a practice quiz. Try taking it before you begin to get a feel for where you stand with knowledge of these principles. Once you've reviewed them, come back and take it again!

Pocket Principles

Let's talk about how to apply these seven Instructional Design Principles to online courses. The following list will give you a brief introduction to each principle. For an in-depth look at the principles, please click the link at the top of this page or within the collapsible sections below. To expand the description of any principle, click or tap the title.

Coherence 🎯

Make sure all materials connect to the learning goals.


Learn more about the Coherence Principle.


Multimedia 🖼️

Use a combination of words and graphics to connect concepts and add visual interest.


Learn more about the Multimedia Principle.

Contiguity 📊

Keep the text near graphics being described.


Learn more about the Contiguity Principle.


Segmenting 📁

Chunk the material into small “bites."


Learn more about the Segmenting Principle.


Redundancy 🔉

Don't repeat in text what you voice in audio.


Learn more about the Redundancy Principle.


Modality 🎤

Opt for audio to balance on-screen elements.


Learn more about the Modality Principle.


Personalization 😎

Use a human voice. Include a human/human-like coach. Be kind with your words.


Learn more about the Personalization Principle.
